Our Research Domain

This research work was created primarily to address the limitations associated with low accuracy and reliability in PD diagnosis models, 'Neura Trace PD' offers a distinctive desktop-based solution. This solution takes a multi-faceted approach, considering four key aspects rather than relying solely on one aspect for diagnosis. By incorporating a range of features or data sources, our aim is to provide more accurate and reliable results in the screening of Parkinson's Disease. This holistic approach can potentially enhance the diagnostic accuracy and reduce the risk of misclassification, ultimately contributing to improved patient care and clinical decision-making.

Screening the Parkinson Disease Using Brain MRI Image

It has been found that observable symptoms of Parkinson’s diseases at early stage include disorders in human’s Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI). The main objective of this is to develop a predictive model that utilizes graph theory and Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) to identify patterns and abnormalities within brain connectivity networks indicative of Parkinson's disease. The identification of different graph matrices and diverse graph connectivity patterns has played a pivotal role in achieving such high accuracy, shedding light on the unique network differences among brain regions. Notably, the model's ability to discern differences in graph connectivity highlights potential insights into the pathophysiology of Parkinson's Disease, such as reduced connectivity in brain regions associated with motor control, offering avenues for targeted treatment strategies. In conclusion, this research component contributes to the field of neuroimaging and Parkinson's disease diagnosis by harnessing the capabilities of graph theory and GCN. By combining MRI imaging, graph representation, and deep learning techniques, and the overall suggest system aim is to improve the accuracy and efficiency of Parkinson's disease screening, ultimately enhancing patient care and quality of life.

Objectives of the research component:

  • Using graph theory to identify connectivity patterns in MRI data that are indicative of Parkinson's disease and normal.
  • Analyze the architecture of GCNs impact their ability to learn and represent complex relationships in brain connectivity networks.
  • Designed web application's user interface to facilitate the seamless upload of MRI scans and the presentation of diagnostic results to patients.

Screening the Parkinson Disease Using Hand Drawing Image

In this study, the researchers introduced a novel method for screening Parkinson's disease (PD) using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), particularly ResNet and DenseNet transfer learning techniques. They used hand-drawn images from individuals with and without PD and trained their models to classify them into PD and non-PD categories. The study also investigated the impact of image resolution on the model's performance. They found that higher resolutions improved accuracy but increased computational complexity. The results showed that CNNs, especially ResNet and DenseNet, were effective in identifying PD from hand-drawn images, achieving good classification accuracy, sensitivity, and specificity. The study's approach offers a non-invasive and accessible screening tool for early PD detection, as hand-drawn images are easy to collect from patients. Moreover, the flexibility to adapt to different image resolutions makes it suitable for various healthcare settings. This research has the potential to significantly improve early PD detection and patient outcomes.

Objectives of the research component:

  • Using hand-drawn spiral images be effectively employed for Parkinson's disease screening using computer vision techniques.
  • Ckecked the performance of a neural network be optimized by determining the optimal image resolution for classification tasks.
  • Discuss solutions we can give due to shortage of medicines.

In many cities around the World Garbage bins or Dustbins are dumped beyond their capacity, and they tend to be ineffective. As the place gets dirtier. Modern techniques for better solid waste segregation, collection, and disposal are the need of the hour.
